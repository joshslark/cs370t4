Statement of purpose :

On February 17, 2015, 7 software teams met in a classroom to interview Andrea Grazzini using a prepared list of questions. Following the interview, our team, The Outsourcers, met in Room B on the Third Floor of Pickler Memorial Library on February 21, 2015 to create this document, a list of requirements for the software project based on the answers Andrea gave to our questions during the interview. This document will precisely define the software project, a bubble map showing civic engagement gaps in the United States, from the perspective of the developer to ensure that the developer's perspective matches the client's.

1. Introduction

  • 1.1 Purpose and Overview of the System
    • 1.1.1 A geographic bubble map of the United States shall be created to illustrate the evolution of civic engagement in the United States.
      (Project Summary Mission)
    • 1.1.2 The target audience shall primarily be corporations, but the map shall also be accessible to the general public
      (Questions E.1, E.2)
  • 1.2 Success Criteria for the System
    • 1.2.1 The map shall display geotagged data sets as bubbles.
      (Project Summary Visual 2.a)
    • 1.2.2 There shall be a key that explains the current state of the data on the map.
      (Project Summary Visual 3)
    • 1.2.3 The filters used on the map shall include Date, Geography, Demography, and Contexts.
      (Project Summary Filters)
    • 1.2.4 The system shall provide predictive data capabilities
      (Questions D.1)
    • 1.2.5 The system shall give authorized users administrative level access to the map
      (Project Summary Visual 1.a)

2. Functional Requirements

  • 2.1 Bubbles
    • 2.1.1 The size of a bubble shall be determined by the size of its corresponding data set.
      (Project Summary Visual 2.a.ii)
    • 2.1.2 No bubble with a data set smaller than another bubble shall have a larger diameter than that bubble.
      (Project Summary 2.a.ii)
    • 2.1.3 The location of a bubble shall be determined by the location in which the data set indicates civic engagement occurred.
      (Project Summary Visual 2.a.i)
  • 2.2 Key
    • 2.2.1 The key shall include a variety of filters that alter the state of the map when clicked.
      (Questions C.1, C.2 C.3)
  • 2.3 Filters
    • 2.3.1 The date filter shall narrow the displayed bubbles to only those that fall within a specified range of years.
      (Project Summary Filters -> Date/Time)
    • 2.3.2 The geography filter shall narrow the displayed bubbles to only those that fall within a certain state or region.
      (Project Summary Filters -> Geography)
    • 2.3.3 The demography filter shall narrow the displayed bubbles to only those that concern people of a certain age, gender, or household income.
      (Project Summary Filters -> Demography)
    • 2.3.4 The contexts filter shall narrow the displayed bubbles to only those that correspond to a specific major civic movement. The major civic movements available to the filter will be suffragists, unions, corporate, anti-slavery, anti-war, prohibition, green, tea party (historical and modern), civil rights (african american and women), LGBT, marriage equity, occupy, presidential administration (name and party), and party in control of congress.
      (Project Summary Filters -> Contexts)
  • 2.4 Administrators
    • 2.4.1 An administrator shall be able to customize the colors of bubbles, borders, and any space on the map that is not covered by an image.
      (Project Summary Visual 1.a.i)
    • 2.4.2 An administrator shall be able to zoom in on the map.
      (Project Summary Visual 1.a.ii)
    • 2.4.3 A regular, unauthorized user shall not be given the above capabilities.
      (Project Summary Visual 1.a)
  • 2.5 Miscellaneous
    • 2.5.1 The starting image shall be a flat, blank map of the United States
      (Questions A.1)
    • 2.5.2 The nodes shall be merge-able if they are connected to one another
      (Questions B.2)

4. Glossary and definitions

  • 4.1 Civic Engagement
    • The encouragement of the general public to become involved in the political process and the issues that affect them.
  • 4.2 Portable
    • The ability to use the software on a variety of devices for the purpose of allowing access from any physical location.
  • 4.3 Node
    • A specific location on the map showing the magnitude of civic engagement in that area.
  • 4.4 Mergeable
    • Nodes on the map show their relationship to each other on the basis of overlapping data
  • 4.5 Administrator
    • A user that is authorized to make changes that a regular user should not be allowed to make.
